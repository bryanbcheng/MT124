Rules for German translation:

(Since verb always the second phrase):
1. If first words in the sentence is not a noun phrase (e.g. preposition like 'mit...' or 'danach'), then bring the last part of the sentence, which should be the subject noun phrase to the beginning

(Genitive)
2. If a noun phrase is followed by a genitive article + another noun phrase, then add 'of the' (e.g. in Teil der Schüler und Schülerinnen)

3. Drop definite articles for big concepts (e.g. 'geht dann auf die Hauptschule' -> 'go to secondary modern school').


4. Prepositions are different for certain concepts (e.g. 'auf' is usually 'on' or 'at' but when we say 'geht dann auf die Hauptschule', we mean 'go to secondary modern school').
